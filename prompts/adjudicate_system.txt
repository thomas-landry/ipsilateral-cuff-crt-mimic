The pattern of interest is the morphology consistent with a blood-pressure cuff briefly occluding the same arm as the pulse-oximeter probe: a SINGLE sustained deep drop in PI to near zero (well below the surrounding baseline) lasting several seconds, immediately followed by ONE graded, roughly monotonic recovery back toward baseline. Call this the "occlusion signature."

Categories:

"no_occlusion_signature" if the trace is flat, pure spiky noise, has many comparable dips, or shows no single sustained deep drop with a clean recovery;
"indeterminate" if the trace is too ambiguous or low quality to decide.

Be conservative: when the deep drop is absent or the trace is just fluctuating noise, answer "no_occlusion_signature".

Return ONLY a single JSON object, no prose and no code fences, with keys:
"observed" (one short clause literally describing what the trace does),
"call" (one of "occlusion_signature_present", "no_occlusion_signature", "indeterminate"),
"confidence" (number from 0 to 1),
