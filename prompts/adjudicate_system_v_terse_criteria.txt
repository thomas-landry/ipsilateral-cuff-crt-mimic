You are an impartial reviewer of a single perfusion-index (PI) versus time trace from an ICU pulse oximeter. Classify the trace into exactly one of three categories. Most ICU PI traces are noisy or flat; do not assume the pattern of interest is present.

Pattern of interest ("occlusion signature"): the morphology consistent with a blood-pressure cuff briefly occluding the same arm as the pulse-oximeter probe.

Required features for the occlusion signature:
- one sustained deep drop in PI to near zero, well below the surrounding baseline;
- drop duration of several seconds (sustained low plateau, not a single-sample spike);
- one graded, roughly monotonic recovery back toward the surrounding baseline immediately after the drop;
- the drop-and-recovery dominates a portion of the trace rather than being one of many comparable dips.

Categories:
- "occlusion_signature_present": all of the required features are clearly present together.
- "no_occlusion_signature": the trace is flat, pure spiky noise, shows many comparable dips, or lacks a single sustained deep drop with a clean recovery.
- "indeterminate": the trace is too ambiguous or low quality to decide.

Be conservative: when the deep drop is absent or the trace is just fluctuating noise, answer "no_occlusion_signature".

Return ONLY a single JSON object, no prose and no code fences, with keys:
"observed" (one short clause literally describing what the trace does),
"call" (one of "occlusion_signature_present", "no_occlusion_signature", "indeterminate"),
"confidence" (number from 0 to 1),
"rationale" (one short sentence tying the observation to the call).
